Volvo S60

The Volvo S60 sedan is well-rounded, with a spacious cabin, upscale interior, and a comfortable ride. Although it’s generally a reliable car, the S60 sedan does run into problems with power steering leaks, and some older cars have issues with the door locks popping up, which requires replacement locks.

Volvo 240

The Volvo 240 was one of the automaker’s most popular cars, and it’s still a gem to find on the used market. The 240 is renowned for its extremely strong and reliable design, with the highest marks for crash safety. Despite the 240’s impressive reliability, it is known for its tendency to blow fuses.

Volvo XC60

The Volvco XC60 is a midsize SUV known for reliability and no major repair issues. However, some years have had malfunctioning cooling fans, and early models often develop a leaking brake vacuum pump at higher mileage.

Common Volvo Repair Issues

Volvo vehicles have strong dependability records and are carefully engineered, but you should still be aware of some common issues. The top ones to look out for include:

  2. Malfunctioning AC system
  3. Oil leaks or clogs
  4. Headlight burnout
