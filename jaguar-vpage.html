Keep Your Jaguar in Top Condition With OEM Parts

Classic Jaguar models are known for their distinctive styling and iconic emblem. The current Jaguar includes crossovers and sedans, and it’s still a strong performance and style icon. Let’s look at a few popular Jaguar models and maintenance issues to watch out for.

Jaguar XJ8

The Jaguar XJ8 is a performance luxury sedan last made for the 2009 model year. It boasts a 4.2-liter V8 with 300 horsepower. The XJ8 was prone to coolant leaks due to the many plastic items under the hood. The thermostat housing and water pump were the biggest culprits for failure.

Jaguar XJ6

A predecessor to the XJ8, the Jaguar XJ6 was also a performance luxury sedan. The XJ6 featured a 4.0-liter six-cylinder engine with 245 horsepower. Common problems included engine oil leaks, rattling noises, and exterior door handles prone to breaking.

Jaguar F-Pace

The Jaguar F-Pace, one of the automaker’s new crossover models, has three engine choices, including a 5.0-liter V8 with 542 horsepower.

Common Jaguar Problems

Jaguar models are packed with power and performance. The top five issues to keep an eye on:

  1. Engine fluid leaks
  2. Electrical issues
  3. Ignition coil failure
  4. Water pump failure
