Dodge Key Features

Engine Options

From the smaller 3.6L Pentastar V6 to the powerhouse 6.2L Supercharged HEMI V8, Dodge has long been known for its diversified engine options and complementary technology like its Multi-Displacement System (MDS) for improved fuel management.

Front, All, and Rear Wheel Drive

Dodge vehicles have several drive system configurations based on model and trim. The Challenger and Charger typically come with rear-wheel drive, whereas the Durango is known for all-wheel drive for improved traction on dry, wet, and snowy roads.

Common Maintenance Issues

To limit downtime, follow a regular maintenance schedule with check-ups to identify potential problems ahead of time:

  • Engine oil leaks
  • Transmission checks
  • Brake pad and rotor replacements
  • Electrical system problems
